Was Magellan worth defending?

After his "contrivertial" journey around the world some are left to wonder if he is worth defending.

ByWyatt Liin
Published March 26th, 2021
• 5 min read

In the 1500's Magellan set sail after prodding many high ranking officals, such as the King of Spain who gave him the go ahead to set sail on September, 20th, 1519. His journey's intent was to find a way through the southern tip of South America from the Atlantic ocean to tha Pacific ocean, the route they took is now known as the Mageallan Strait, and was used for hundreds of years as the main shipping route through South America.

His journey reached its goal, but his crew was working in poor conditions and had little to no food by the end of the trip. His crew ate rats, pee soaked biscits, and even leather. Hard tack was their main food source untill one of their other ships the San Antonio, left them, the San Antonio was their main supply ship so that made the rest of the trip much harder.

The conditions on the trip were so bad that some of his crew, on multiple occasions tried to mutany. And he was good at being a captain with the information he had, but he had little control over his crew. So when a second mutany happened he killed the leaders of the mutany. This gave him more control over the crew he had left.

Later on in the journey they were passing some islands and decided to stop by and massacre a small village. This lead to captain Magellan's death. After he died what was left of his crew drove the ships home.

There was about 250 people that left on his voyage and only 18 returned. Many died from scurvey, which is caused when there is a lack of vitamin c, but Magellan and his other higher ranking people on his ship did not, because they ate a jello as a desert that had enough vitamin c to keep them alive. In a part of the trip they passed the most violent part of the ocean, causing them to spend 8 months sailing through a small area.

So overall He was a good captain but bad at keeping his crew in line, they tried to revolt twice. If he was better at keeping his crew in line he would have had a better journey overall. If you were a crew member he would not be the person worth defending but as a outside observer yes, he would be because he would be finding new trade routes that could help expand the global spice trade.
